What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. Human-driven emissions of greenhouse gases are the main driver of the rapid warming observed since the 20th century.

  • Fossil fuel burning
  • Deforestation
  • Industrial agriculture
  • Urbanization & transport

Quick facts

  • Human activities increased atmospheric CO‚ÇÇ by over 50% since 1750.
  • Recent years are the warmest in at least the last 125,000 years.
  • Mitigation and adaptation together reduce impacts and costs.

Take Action

Simple steps add up ‚Äî reduce energy use, choose low-carbon transport, eat less meat, support reforestation, and vote for strong climate policies.

‚ö° Energy Saving Tips

  • Switch to LEDs: They use 75% less energy than incandescent lighting.
  • Unplug Electronics: "Vampire power" drains energy even when devices are off.
  • Cold Water Wash: 90% of washing machine energy goes to heating water.
  • Smart Thermostat: Regulate heating/cooling automatically to save ~10% annually.
  • Seal the Gaps: Weatherstrip windows and doors to stop drafts.

‚ö†Ô∏è Future Consequences

If warming exceeds 1.5¬∞C, the risks increase significantly:

  • Extreme Weather: More frequent and severe hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves.
  • Biodiversity Loss: Up to 14% of species face very high risk of extinction.
  • Water Scarcity: 2 billion people could face water stress.
  • Food Security: Yields of maize, rice, and wheat will likely decline globally.
  • Economic Impact: Global GDP could drop by 11-14% by 2050 if unchecked.
